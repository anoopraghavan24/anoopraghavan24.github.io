PART I: THE ASSET (2012 FORD F-550)

Mechanical Architecture

The 2012 F-550 is a Class 5 Medium-Duty chassis. This is a professional-grade platform designed for 250,000+ mile service lives in high-stress environments.

Engine: 6.7L PowerStroke V8

300 HP / 660 Lb-Ft Torque. Features a "Reverse-Flow" cooling system and a CGI (Compacted Graphite Iron) block for extreme durability.

Chassis: 19,500 Lb GVWR

The frame is a straight-rail design, 50k PSI steel. This allows for massive payload capacity for a full camper conversion.

Charging: Dual Alternators

Standard on most shuttle builds. Provides ~300+ Amps of current, perfect for charging large "house" battery banks while driving.

Transmission: 6R140 TorqShift

Heavy-duty 6-speed with a double-overdrive and "Tow/Haul" mode for effective engine braking on descents.

Mandatory Pre-Flight Checklist

  • The "Glitter Test": Open the primary fuel filter housing. Inspect for metallic debris (indicates CP4 pump wear).
  • Tire Date Codes: Verify the 4-digit DOT code. If the tires are older than 2020, they are considered "at-risk" for a 5-hour highway haul.
  • DEF Status: Ensure the Diesel Exhaust Fluid tank is at least 50% full. Low DEF will trigger a 50 MPH speed governor.
  • Coolant Inspection: Verify the color is Gold/Yellow. Inspect the degas bottle for "oil film," which would indicate a failing oil cooler.
