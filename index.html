🔧 Important Vehicle Notes
🚛
6.7L PowerStroke Diesel — Pre-Trip Check
Photo imgi_78 confirms the big PowerStroke diesel truck. Before leaving, check the Primary Fuel Filter for metallic "glitter" particles (a sign of pump wear). Make sure the Diesel Exhaust Fluid (DEF) tank is topped off — running low puts the truck into Limp Mode!
🚧
Fiberglass Damage & Rainy Day Driving
Photo imgi_84 shows some damage to the rear fiberglass — keep an eye on it, especially on bumpy roads. Wednesday has a 45% rain chance, so take it extra easy with this 19,500 lb truck — leave plenty of following distance and slow down! 🌧️

🔍 First-Sight Inspection Checklist

⚠️ Do ALL of these BEFORE signing the final release or starting the engine!

TaskWhat to Check⏱ Time
🪣 The "Glitter" Test Pull the secondary fuel filter; check the bowl for metal shavings — indicates HPFP failure. 15 min
💧 Fluid Levels Check Oil, Coolant (look for "white crust" leaks), and Brake fluid. 10 min
🛞 Tire Condition Check for dry rot in sidewalls and set pressure to 85–95 PSI (standard F-550 load). 15 min
🔩 Under-Chassis Look for active drips from the transmission or differential. 5 min
🔋 Battery Health Check for corrosion on terminals; ensure the bus cranks "healthy" and fast. 5 min
🐍 Belts / Hoses Squeeze radiator hoses (shouldn't be brittle) and check serpentine belt for cracks. 10 min
🛠️ On-Site Maintenance & Packing Guide

If the bus needs immediate service at the warehouse:

🔩 Fuel Filter Change (Primary & Secondary)
Est. Time30–45 minutes NoteThe 6.7L has two filters. Primary is on the frame rail (under driver's side); secondary is on top of the engine. ⚠️ Crucial!You MUST cycle the key to "ON" (do not start) 6–8 times for 30 seconds each to prime the system. Skipping this step can damage the high-pressure fuel pump!
🛢️ Oil Change
Est. Time45 minutes NoteThe F-550 holds approximately 13 quarts — make sure you have a large enough drain pan!
💧 DEF Refill
Est. Time5 minutes ⚠️ WarningIf the bus is under 1/4 tank of DEF, it may enter "Limp Mode" shortly after hitting the highway — limiting speed to 50 MPH or even 5 MPH!

Pro-Tip — The Idle Test
Before leaving the warehouse, let the bus idle for at least 15 minutes while watching the temperature gauge. If it stays stable at idle, it is far more likely to handle the 3-hour trek back to Louisiana safely!
