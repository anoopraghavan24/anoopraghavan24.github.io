2012 Ford F-550 Shuttle Bus

Mechanical & Chassis Specification Briefing

I. Powertrain Architecture

The 6.7L PowerStroke was Ford's first in-house diesel engine, engineered to deliver massive torque at low RPMs for heavy-duty shuttle applications.

Engine 6.7L PowerStroke V8 Turbo Diesel
Output 300 HP / 660 Lb-Ft Torque
Transmission TorqShift 6-Speed Auto (6R140)
Emissions SCR w/ Diesel Exhaust Fluid (DEF)

II. Chassis & Structural

Built on a Class 5 Medium-Duty frame, this chassis is significantly more robust than consumer-grade F-250/F-350 trucks.

GVWR 19,500 Lbs (Commercial Grade)
Frame Style Straight-Channel Steel
Charging Dual Heavy-Duty Alternators
Rear Axle Dana Spicer S110 (Dual Rear Wheels)

III. Pre-Flight Mechanical Checklist

  • Fluid Integrity: Check Oil (15W-40) and Coolant (Ford Gold). Ensure DEF tank is at least 50% full.
  • The "Glitter" Test: Inspect primary fuel filter housing for metallic flakes (indicates fuel pump health).
  • Tire Age: Verify DOT date codes. If older than 6 years, exercise extreme caution on the highway.
  • Electrical Drain: Test dual batteries for 12.6V charge; check alternator output after startup.
  • Braking Surface: Inspect rotors for deep pitting or uneven wear from extended sitting.
Conversion Note: The F-550's flat-rail frame allows for mounting grey-water tanks, diesel heaters, and storage boxes directly to the frame rails beneath the body, making it the ideal platform for a high-end camper build.
